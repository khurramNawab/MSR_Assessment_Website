Compliance Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

To measure the effectiveness of the Integrated Management System, organizations must track specific, quantitative KPIs. During surveillance audits, registrars inspect these metrics to verify continual improvement:

  • First-Pass Yield (FPY): Measures the percentage of products completed without defects or rework, reflecting process quality.
  • Vulnerability Closure Time: For IT/SaaS entities, tracking the average hours to remediate critical security vulnerabilities.
  • Incident Frequency Rate (IFR): For construction and manufacturing, monitoring safety incidents per 100,000 man-hours worked.
  • Supplier Quality Index (SQI): Evaluating subcontractor and vendor compliance logs to maintain supply chain security.

Standard Audit Documentation Checklist

To facilitate Stage 1 and Stage 2 registrar evaluations, our consulting desk helps you organize your evidence library. Below is the standard list of folders and operational logs that must be prepared and locked before the assessor's visit:

  • Management Review Minutes (MRM): A complete record of the annual management review meeting signed by directors. This includes reviews of quality objectives, internal audit results, customer feedback, and process improvement logs.

Understanding ISO 28000 and ISO 9001 in Logistics

Logistics networks operate across expansive geographical zones managing valuable client inventory. High-risk exposure during transit, warehouse thefts, or cargo damage can disrupt corporate supply pipelines. To address these liabilities, two primary ISO structures serve as core governance:

1. ISO 28000:2022 (Security Management System for the Supply Chain)

Provides a systematic security framework for transport and supply chain operators. Audits evaluate physical security barriers at warehouses, logical cargo tracking systems, container integrity checks (high-security seals), employee background checks, and emergency response plans during hijackings or losses.

2. ISO 9001:2015 (Quality Management System for Transport Operators)

Standardizes service delivery performance. It ensures structured SOPs for fleet maintenance (checking tire and engine safety parameters), driver transit hour logs to prevent fatigue, customs documentation checklists, and customer delivery dispute registers.

Who Benefits from Supply Chain Compliance Manuals?

Accredited security systems are critical for all logistics operators to secure corporate business:

  • Third-Party Logistics (3PL & 4PL) Providers: Storing and shipping high-value corporate inventories.
  • Warehousing & Fulfillment Centers: Managing massive storage facilities requiring robust access security systems.
  • Customs Brokers & Freight Forwarders: Handling statutory compliance filings at airports and seaports.
  • Cold Chain Fleet Operators: Delivering temperature-sensitive pharmaceutical or food goods.

Logistics Compliance FAQs

ISO 28000 is an international standard specifically addressing security management systems in supply chains. It helps transport operators identify security risks (such as cargo theft or sabotage), implement controls, and prove to large corporate shippers that their goods are secure during storage and transit.
Granted by Indian Customs, Authorized Economic Operator (AEO) status is a security compliance certification indicating that an international trade operator conforms to WCO security standards, qualifying them for faster customs clearance and lower bank guarantee rates.
Under ISO 9001, firms design SOPs regulating driver transit hours (e.g. max 8 hours driving without a break), mandatory safety training registers, and vehicle inspection checklists (brakes, tires, cargo strap integrity checks), reducing accidents.
