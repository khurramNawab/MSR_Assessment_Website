Our Accredited Auditing Methodology

We conduct audits following standardized verification frameworks based on international best practices. Our methodology ensures that certifications are not merely transactions but strategic process improvements:

Stage 1: Document Review

Before site audits occur, our assessors review your written processes, policies, standard guides, and manuals. We identify deviations from standard clauses (e.g., ISO 9001 Section 8 or ISO 27001 Annex A) and issue a Readiness Report outlining required rectifications.

Stage 2: Operational Auditing

Our auditors inspect your operations, test workflows, audit safety logs, and interview process managers. We gather objective evidence to verify that documented policies are implemented in daily workflows, generating a formal Audit Report.

CAPA Optimization Cycle

If any non-conformances (NCs) are logged during Stage 2, our advisory desk supports your team in performing root-cause analysis and establishing Corrective and Preventive Actions (CAPA) before registrar signoff.

Ongoing Surveillance Audits

An ISO certification demands continuous commitment. In Year 1 and Year 2 post-certification, we execute surveillance inspections to ensure process standards do not decline, preserving operational compliance.

Editorial & Integrity Guidelines

Regulatory compliance requires absolute precision. A minor error in interpreting tax tables, security policies, or environment safety regulations can have legal implications for business owners. Therefore, MSR Assessment implements a strict Editorial Policy:

  • Citations of Authority: Every article must cite official publications from regulatory bodies like the MCA, Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC), ISO, or legal acts.
  • Lead Auditor Review: Standard guides are checked by a lead auditor in the corresponding standard to ensure process roadmaps are practically correct.
  • Dynamic Review Cycle: Content is re-reviewed every 6 months to account for statutory adjustments, amendments, and registry portal upgrades (e.g. MCA V3 upgrades).

Compliance Review Process

Drafting: Written by CA, CS, or legal consultants based on official law drafts.
Auditor Review: Peer reviewed by a Lead Auditor for methodology alignment.
Verification check: Checked for broken links, old portal screenshots, or obsolete forms.
Accreditation Signoff: Published with last updated stamp and Auditor ID credentials.
